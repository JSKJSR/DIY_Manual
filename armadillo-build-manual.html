Getting Started

Before You Begin

Read this section completely before touching a single bolt. These concepts appear throughout every phase of the build.

The Two-Wrench Technique

Every time you tighten a nut onto a bolt, you must hold the bolt head still with one spanner while turning the nut with a second. If you spin only the nut, the bolt rotates too and you won't achieve the correct torque — the joint will be loose.

Thread Lock Guide

Loctite 222 — Purple/Weak
Brake rotor bolts, small M5 fasteners. Removable with hand tools after curing.
Loctite 243 — Blue/Medium
Seat rails, chainring bolts, Rohloff sprocket. Requires heat to remove.
No thread-lock
Suspension pivot bolts (use nylon-stop nuts instead), wheel axle nuts, bumpers.

Glossary

Rod End
A ball joint on a threaded shank. Allows angular movement in all directions at a pivot point.
Bellow
Rubber accordion boot that protects a rod end ball joint from dirt and moisture.
Distance Bush
Metal spacer cylinder placed inside a wishbone hole to prevent over-clamping the silent block.
Silent Block
Rubber-bonded metal bushing pressed into the frame. Absorbs vibration at suspension pivot points.
Cardan Shaft
Universal-joint driveshaft. Transmits rotation through an angle — allows suspension movement without disconnecting the drive.
Parallel Key
Rectangular steel prism that locks a sprocket or gear onto an axle by engaging matching slots in both parts.
Nylon-Stop Nut
A nut with a nylon insert that grips the thread to resist loosening. Single-use only — replace every time.
Torque
Rotational tightening force measured in Newton-metres (Nm). Too little = joint loosens. Too much = stripped threads.
Toe
Whether front wheels point slightly inward (toe-in) or outward (toe-out). Correct toe keeps the bike tracking straight.
BB (Bottom Bracket)
The threaded shell in the frame/boom that the pedal crankset axle or Bafang motor threads into.
Double Wishbone
Suspension design using two triangular arms per wheel corner (upper + lower), giving independent wheel movement.
Bayonet Connector
Push-and-twist locking connector used on the Rohloff hub axle ends and shift cables — allows tool-free wheel removal.

Bill of Materials

Know Your Parts

Pick up each part and match it to a card before starting the build. Label mirrored pairs with masking tape immediately.

0074

Main Frame

The welded aluminium backbone of the entire bike. Every other component attaches to it.

Identify it: Largest single piece. Four large circular holes at corners for suspension arms.

⚠️
Never drop or bend the frame. Factory alignment is precision-set.
0068

Front Boom

Telescoping tube holding the pedals/motor out in front of the rider. Slide to set leg length.

⚠️
Never ride with clamp bolt loose — boom can slide forward under load.
0020 / 0021

Kingpin Assembly

Vertical pivot bolt for each front wheel's steering. 0020 = Left (left-thread). 0021 = Right.

⚠️
0020 tightens counter-clockwise (left-hand thread).
0072–0076

Wishbone Arms ×8

Triangular arms connecting each wheel to the frame. Two per corner: upper + lower = "double wishbone" suspension.

ℹ️
Label FL/FR/RL/RR Upper and Lower with tape immediately on unboxing.
0017 / 0018 / 0019

Rod Ends

Ball joint on a threaded shank. 0017 (M10) = suspension. 0018 (M8 left-thread) and 0019 (M8 right-thread) = steering.

⚠️
0018 has a groove on the hex flats = left-hand thread. Tightens counter-clockwise.
0022

Bellows

Rubber accordion boot protecting the rod end ball joint. Insert through the SIDE SLIT — not the end hole.

ℹ️
A dab of dish soap on the rubber makes fitting much easier.
DR8 / DR10

Distance Bushes

Short metal spacer inside the wishbone hole. Prevents over-clamping the silent block rubber.

⚠️
Missing distance bush = crushed rubber = suspension failure.
0142

Shock Absorbers ×4

Hydraulic damper, one per wheel corner. Body UP, piston rod DOWN. Inverted = oil in wrong chamber, no damping.

Torque eye bolts: 25 Nm.

0192

Cardan Shaft ×2

Universal-joint driveshaft transmitting power to the rear wheels. Contains an internal coil spring that MUST be present.

⚠️
Shake shaft before fitting. You must feel spring resistance. No spring = shaft disconnects under load.
6×6×20 mm

Parallel Keys

Small steel prism locking sprockets/gears to axles. Grease both slots and key before fitting to prevent fretting.

0008 + 0069 + 0088

Freewheel Assembly

Lets you coast without pedalling. Pre-assemble on bench: press greased bearings into housing, slide axle through, fit circlip.

0015

Bafang Max Motor

250W torque-sensing mid-drive motor. Threads into the front boom BB shell. Drive side right, cable exits left.

Torque: 17–18 Nm with BB splined cup tool.

Rohloff 500/14

14-Speed Hub

14-speed oil-bath gearbox in the rear wheel. Two shift cables, bayonet axle ends, twist-grip shifter only.

⚠️
Sprocket torque 40 Nm — highest in the entire build. Use calibrated torque wrench.
Tektro Hydraulic

Disc Brakes ×4

Hydraulic disc brakes with mineral oil. Never use DOT fluid — destroys seals immediately.

Rotor bolts: 4–5 Nm with Loctite 222. Pad clearance: 0.2–0.3 mm each side.

Battery + 0047

Battery & Mount

Lithium-ion pack powering the Bafang motor. Mount as low as possible for better handling. Voltage must match motor.

⚠️
Red = positive (+). Black = negative (−). Never reverse. Never short the terminals.
0082

Chain Tensioner

Spring-loaded arm pressing against the chain to maintain tension as the suspension moves. 5–10mm sag at midpoint between sprockets.

0003 / 0013

Chainrings

0003 (42T) mounts on the Bafang motor spider. 0013 (38T) mounts on the freewheel assembly. Both use Loctite 243 on chainring bolts.

1
Verify & Clean
Estimated time: 1–2 hours · No assembly in this phase
1

Unpack and inventory every part

Parts ListMarker penMasking tape

Lay everything on a clean flat surface. Go through the Parts List row by row and tick each part as you find it. For matched pairs (left/right wishbones, etc.) label them immediately: "FL Upper", "FR Upper", "RL Lower" etc. Do this before anything else — once parts are mixed up they are nearly impossible to separate.

✅
Why this matters: A missing part discovered mid-build means complete disassembly and weeks of waiting. Catching it now takes two minutes.
2

Inspect the main frame

0074 Main FrameBright torch

Examine every weld, tube junction, and hole. Sight down each tube like a rifle barrel — any bend is visible. Look for: cracks at weld joints, stripped bolt holes, burrs or sharp edges (file smooth if found).

⚠️
Stop if you find a crack. A damaged frame is invisible once the bike is built and will fail dangerously. Contact Velove for a replacement.
3

Chase all threaded holes

Thread tap set M5/M6/M8/M10Wire brush

Run a matching tap through every bolt hole in the frame to clear paint, powder coat, or machining debris from the threads. New frames routinely have partially blocked threads from the coating process. A blocked thread makes a bolt feel tight when it isn't — a dangerous false reading.

ℹ️
Thread tap sets are inexpensive at any hardware store. You need M5, M6, M8, M10.
4

Press silent blocks into frame

0026/0027 Silent Blocks ×8Bench viceSocket (matching OD)
  1. Hold silent block over frame hole — rubber side enters first
  2. Press by hand until it starts to seat
  3. Use vice or socket (same OD as silent block) to press flush
  4. Verify: must be fully flush with frame face, must not spin in the hole
⚠️
Do not hammer directly. Use a flat socket to distribute pressing force or the rubber tears. A spinning silent block was not pressed fully — re-seat before bolting.
5

Grease all rod end threads

All rod ends (0017/0018/0019)Copper anti-seize grease

Apply a thin smear of copper anti-seize to the threaded shanks of all rod ends. This prevents thread galling (metal cold-welding under load) and makes future alignment adjustments possible. Do NOT grease the ball joint portion — grease there attracts dirt and accelerates wear.

2
Suspension Pre-Assembly
Estimated time: 3–4 hours · All 4 wheel corners
1

Fit rod ends and bellows into all wishbone arms

All 8 wishbone arms0017 Rod Ends ×160022 Bellows ×1615mm + 17mm spanners
  1. Slide bellow onto rod end shank through SIDE SLIT (not the end hole)
  2. Screw rod end into wishbone arm end — hand tight to start
  3. Two-wrench technique to 25 Nm: hold bolt head still, turn nut
  4. Pull bellow over the joint body to cover it completely
⚠️
If the rod end requires more than light finger pressure to start threading, stop. It is cross-threaded. Back out and re-align before continuing.
2

Mount lower wishbones to frame

4× Lower WishbonesDR10 Distance Bushes ×8M10 Bolts + Nylon-Stop NutsTorque wrench
  1. Work one corner at a time (Front-Left first)
  2. Insert DR10 distance bush into silent block centre hole
  3. Align lower wishbone with frame bracket
  4. Insert M10 bolt: frame → distance bush → wishbone pivot
  5. Fit a NEW nylon-stop nut (never re-use)
  6. Leave finger-tight until wheel assembly is also connected
  7. Repeat for all 4 lower arms. Final torque 35 Nm once all three attachment points are connected
ℹ️
Tighten to full torque only once all three points of the wishbone are connected. Tightening with only two points attached twists the arm and causes binding.
3

Mount upper wishbones to frame

0072/0073/0075/0076 Upper ArmsDR8 Distance Bushes ×8M8 Bolts + Nylon-Stop Nuts

Same procedure as lower wishbones using M8 hardware and DR8 distance bushes. Upper arms attach to the frame bracket directly above the lower arm. Final torque 25 Nm.

⚠️
Upper arms are shorter than lower arms. Mixing them makes the suspension geometry wrong and causes binding.
4

Fit wheels to hub carriers

0397 Wheels ×4M10 pivot bolts35 Nm torque wrench

Connect the wheel hub carrier to both the upper and lower wishbone rod ends using the hub pivot bolt. Fit nylon-stop nuts. Torque 35 Nm. Once wheels are fitted, go back and torque all wishbone frame-pivot bolts to their final values (M8: 25 Nm, M10: 35 Nm).

5

Install shock absorbers

0142 Shocks ×4M8 Eye Bolts + Nylon-Stop Nuts25 Nm torque wrench
  1. Orient: damper body UP, piston rod DOWN — never invert
  2. Insert upper eye bolt through frame bracket — torque 25 Nm
  3. Insert lower eye bolt through lower wishbone bracket — torque 25 Nm
  4. Push down on the corner: shock must compress and rebound slowly
✅
Why body-up: Hydraulic oil must be in the lower chamber around the piston. Inversion puts oil above the piston — the damper becomes a spring with no damping.
6

Suspension bounce test

Push down firmly on each corner. Each should: compress smoothly without binding, rebound slowly (not spring back instantly), and return to the same height. If a corner springs back instantly, the shock is inverted. If it binds, the wishbone pivot bolt was over-tightened with the arm in a twisted position — loosen, allow arm to relax naturally, re-torque.

3
Kingpins & Steering
Estimated time: 2–3 hours · Front wheels will steer after this phase
1

Grease and insert kingpins

0020 Left Kingpin0021 Right KingpinLithium bearing greaseRubber mallet
  1. Pack all bearing surfaces with grease — use finger or brush
  2. Insert kingpin from TOP through steering knuckle and upper bearing
  3. Tap gently with rubber mallet through lower bearing
  4. Thread top nut: left kingpin (0020) uses left-hand thread — turns counter-clockwise
  5. Torque top nut 30 Nm, bottom nut 30 Nm (nylon-stop, new each time)
  6. Verify: wheel pivots smoothly side-to-side with zero stiffness or play
⚠️
0020 left kingpin tightens counter-clockwise. Forcing clockwise strips the thread permanently. Verify thread direction before applying any torque.
2

Install steering rack

Steering rack assemblyM8 mounting bolts

Bolt the central steering rack to the frame centre bracket. Torque mounting bolts to 25 Nm. Centre the rack: measure from rack centre to each frame side — these must be equal within 2 mm. An off-centre rack gives unequal steering lock left and right.

3

Fit steering rod ends (0018 + 0019)

0018 M8 Left-thread ×10019 M8 Right-thread ×1Steering rods ×20022 Bellows

Each steering rod uses one 0018 (rack end) and one 0019 (wheel end). This opposite-thread arrangement is intentional: rotating the turnbuckle barrel changes the rod length to adjust wheel alignment. Set initial engagement: screw both rod ends to equal depth (~15 mm). Final alignment is set in Phase 5.

⚠️
Never fit two identical-thread rod ends on the same steering rod. The turnbuckle only works with one of each.
4

Connect steering rods to kingpins and rack

Bolt rod end ball joints to the kingpin steering arm and to the rack. M8 bolts with flat washers and nylon-stop nuts. Torque 20 Nm. Leave turnbuckle lock nuts loose — alignment is set in Phase 5. Turn handlebars full-lock both ways: all rods must move freely with no cable or hose contact.

4
Freewheel, Bafang & Drivetrain
Estimated time: 3–4 hours · Drivetrain complete after this phase
1

Pre-assemble the freewheel unit

0088 Housing0008 Bearings ×20069 AxleBearing greaseBench vice + socket
  1. Pack both bearings (0008) with grease — work grease between all balls
  2. Place housing on vice, flat side down. Set first bearing squarely in hole — must be perfectly level
  3. Press using socket matching the bearing outer race diameter until flush
  4. Flip housing, repeat for second bearing on opposite side
  5. Slide axle (0069) through both bearings, fit circlip into axle groove
  6. Check: axle spins smoothly, housing does NOT spin on axle
⚠️
A tilted bearing binds and wears out within weeks. Take your time getting it perfectly square before pressing. Once pressed in wrong, it must be destroyed to remove.
2

Install Bafang Max motor

0015 Bafang Max 250W0068 Front BoomBB splined cup tool17–18 Nm torque wrenchAnti-seize grease
  1. Clean BB shell threads in the front boom
  2. Apply thin film of anti-seize to BB thread (not the motor itself)
  3. Thread motor in from drive side (right) — hand tight only at first
  4. Use BB tool to torque: 17–18 Nm
  5. Route motor cable through boom clamp toward controller position
ℹ️
If threading feels stiff at any point, stop and re-align. The thread specification is BSA 68 mm or 73 mm — verify your boom matches before starting.
3

Install 42T chainring on motor

0003 Chainring 42TChainring bolts ×5Loctite 2438–10 Nm torque wrench
  1. Align chainring spider holes with Bafang spider arms
  2. Apply one small drop of Loctite 243 to each bolt thread
  3. Thread all 5 bolts in by hand before tightening any
  4. Tighten in star pattern (1 → opposite → next → opposite → last)
  5. Final torque: 8–10 Nm each
  6. Spin by hand — max 1 mm wobble. More = undertightened bolt
4

Install cardan shafts ×2

0192 Cardan Shafts ×26×6×20 mm Parallel Keys ×4Copper grease
  1. Shake each cardan shaft — verify internal spring is present (you feel slight resistance at the joint)
  2. Clean axle key slots and cardan shaft key slots
  3. Apply thin grease film to parallel key and both slots
  4. Press key into AXLE slot — snug fit, not sloppy
  5. Align cardan shaft key slot with the key and push shaft onto axle
  6. Verify: shaft rotates with axle, does not spin freely
⚠️
No spring = catastrophic failure. A shaft without its internal spring disconnects under load causing sudden total loss of drive. This check takes 30 seconds. Do it every time.
5

Mount freewheel and 38T chainring

Bolt the complete pre-assembled freewheel unit to the frame bracket. Mount the 38T chainring (0013) to the freewheel axle using a parallel key (same procedure as Step 4). The cardan shaft will connect the freewheel axle to the Rohloff hub in Phase 5 — do not connect it yet.

6

Install chain idlers (0006 + 0007) and tensioner (0082)

0006 Upper Idler0007 Lower Idler0082 Chain TensionerM8 bolts · 15 Nm

Mount each idler on its frame tab, torque 15 Nm. After tightening each idler: spin it by hand — must spin freely. If it binds, back off the bolt slightly; the bearing inner race is being compressed. Mount tensioner on its frame tab with spring arm facing the chain path. Do not tension against the chain yet — chain is installed in Phase 6.

5
Rohloff, Alignment & Brakes
Estimated time: 3–4 hours
1

Install Rohloff hub and sprocket

Rohloff Speedhub 500/14Rear right wheelRohloff sprocket tool40 Nm torque wrenchLoctite 243
  1. Seat hub into rear right wheel (drive side)
  2. Apply Loctite 243 to sprocket thread — LEFT-HAND thread, counter-clockwise to tighten
  3. Use Rohloff sprocket tool to torque: 40 Nm
  4. Engage bayonet axle ends in rear axle slots — push and twist to lock
  5. Torque wheel axle nuts: 35 Nm
⚠️
40 Nm is the highest torque value in this build. An under-torqued sprocket unscrews in use and destroys the hub threading — a €500+ repair.
2

Connect cardan shaft to Rohloff hub

Connect the cardan shaft between the freewheel axle and Rohloff hub axle using the parallel key procedure from Phase 4 Step 4. Double-check the internal spring is present before connecting — it is easy to dislodge when handling the shaft.

3

Route and tension Rohloff shift cables

Rohloff Twist Shifter2× Shift cables (different lengths)2× Cable outer housingCable crimps ×4
  1. Mount twist-grip shifter on handlebar — M3 bolts, 3 Nm
  2. Route cable outer housing from shifter along frame to Rohloff right side panel
  3. Rohloff has two cable ports: shorter cable → top port, longer cable → lower port
  4. Connect inner wires to bayonet connectors (push and twist)
  5. Set tension with bike in gear 4: cables just taut with no sag
  6. Crimp end caps on all cut cable ends
  7. Test: shift through all 14 gears while pedalling slowly
⚠️
Rohloff supplies two cables of different lengths. Do not cut both to the same length — the routing geometry requires the asymmetry. One gear direction will fail to engage if cables are equal length.
4

Set wheel alignment (toe)

Tape measureTwo 15 mm spannersSteering rod turnbuckles
  1. Set steering to straight-ahead (centred)
  2. Measure front edge gap between both front tyres
  3. Measure rear edge gap between same two tyres
  4. Target: front measurement = rear, or front 1–2 mm smaller (toe-in)
  5. Adjust: loosen turnbuckle lock nuts → rotate barrel to change rod length → re-measure
  6. Tighten lock nuts to 20 Nm when correct
✅
Why toe-in matters: Even 3 mm of toe-out causes tyres to scrub sideways, destroying them in 50 km and making the bike pull to one side.
5

Fit brake rotors

4× Disc rotorsTorx T25Loctite 2224–5 Nm torque wrench

Each rotor: 6 Torx T25 bolts. One drop of Loctite 222 per bolt. Tighten in star pattern to 4–5 Nm. Rotor bolts are small — over-torque strips them instantly. After fitting, spin each wheel edge-on: rotor should run true. A wobble means an undertightened bolt — re-torque in star pattern.

6

Mount and centre brake calipers

4× Tektro calipersAdaptor plates (if required)Loctite 2228–10 Nm torque wrench
  1. If adaptor plates needed: fit with Loctite 222, torque 8–10 Nm
  2. Mount caliper loosely on adaptor/frame (do not tighten yet)
  3. Insert folded card between pads to centre caliper over rotor
  4. Squeeze brake lever firmly — pads press against the card, centering the caliper
  5. While holding lever: tighten caliper bolts to 8–10 Nm
  6. Release lever, remove card, spin wheel — rotor must clear pads on both sides

Front Boom, Chain, Battery & Final
Estimated time: 3–5 hours · Bike is rideable after this phase
1

Level and clamp the front boom

0068 Front BoomSpirit level8–10 Nm torque wrench
  1. Insert boom clamp end into frame socket
  2. Sit in seat: adjust boom length until slight knee bend at pedal bottom
  3. Place spirit level on top of boom — adjust until perfectly horizontal
  4. Tighten clamp bolt to 8–10 Nm
  5. Try to push or rotate boom by hand: it must not move
⚠️
A tilted boom changes the pedalling angle and causes knee strain. Never skip the spirit level step.
2

Install main chain (motor to freewheel)

Main chainChain toolMaster link
  1. Route: over 42T front chainring → over upper idler (0006) → under lower idler (0007) → onto 38T freewheel ring
  2. Big-big+2 method: wrap chain around largest front and rear rings, pull ends snug, add 2 links
  3. Cut to length with chain tool
  4. Connect with master link — both side plates must click fully closed
  5. Engage chain tensioner (0082) — 5–10 mm sag midpoint
  6. Spin pedals by hand: chain stays on all sprockets, no rubbing
3

Install second chain (freewheel to Rohloff)

Shorter chain connecting freewheel output to Rohloff hub sprocket. Route directly between the two. Same big-big+2 sizing method, second master link. Spin rear wheel by hand — both chains should run smoothly with no chattering or jumping. Any jumping indicates a stiff link or incorrect length.

4

Mount the battery

Battery pack0047 Mount bracketM6 bolts ×4

Bolt mount bracket (0047) at the lowest viable frame position, centred left-right. Clip or slide battery onto bracket. Secure any anti-rattle straps. Do not connect the power connector yet — all other wiring must be routed first.

⚠️
Mount battery as low as possible. A high battery raises the centre of gravity and makes the bike tip in corners.
5

Connect wiring loom

Follow the Wiring Guide section. Connect motor, display, brake cut-offs, speed sensor, and lights. Cable-tie everything — no wire should hang free or touch a moving part. Final step: connect the battery connector (XT60/XT90), red to red, black to black. Display should illuminate immediately.

⚠️
Reversed motor connector = immediate controller damage. Match colours before connecting. If in doubt, do not connect.
6

Install lights and bumpers

0364 Headlight0554 Taillight0361 Indicators ×40574/0575 Bumpers

Mount headlight (front boom/nose), taillight (rear frame), indicators (each corner). Connect each to loom. M5 bolts, 3–4 Nm — plastic brackets crack easily. Test every light: headlight forward, indicators flash amber, taillight red. Bolt bumpers last: M6 bolts, 8 Nm, no thread-lock.

7

Static pre-ride inspection

Before any movement, verify: all suspension bolts torqued · all 4 wheels spin freely · no brake drag · both chains correct tension · all cables away from moving parts · battery connector secure · steering smooth lock to lock · all lights on · tyres at rated pressure. Perform the bounce test one final time — all 4 corners must rebound at equal speed.

✅
First 500m test: Ride slowly in a safe area before any speed. Test braking, steering, and motor assist one at a time before combining them.

Hydraulic System

Brake Bleeding

Tektro hydraulic brakes use mineral oil exclusively. One wrong fluid choice destroys all four brake systems simultaneously.

⚠️
MINERAL OIL ONLY. Never use DOT brake fluid in Tektro brakes. DOT fluid dissolves the seals within hours. The bottle must say "Hydraulic Mineral Oil" — check before opening.
1

Prepare the caliper end

Remove wheel. Remove brake pads and insert a bleed block (or folded card) between the pistons to prevent them being pushed out. Locate the bleed nipple on the caliper body — usually on the inboard side.

2

Prepare the lever reservoir

Rotate the handlebar so the brake lever reservoir is level (horizontal). Remove the reservoir cover and bleed screw. The reservoir is under a rubber cap on the lever body.

3

Fill and push from caliper upward

Fill syringe with mineral oil — tap it and push air out first. Attach syringe tube to caliper bleed nipple. Open nipple 1/4 turn. Push oil slowly upward through the hose. Air bubbles rise to the reservoir.

4

Watch the reservoir

Continue pushing until no more bubbles emerge at the reservoir. Top up the reservoir as needed — it must not run dry or air is pulled back in.

5

Close and test

Close caliper nipple first (before removing syringe). Replace reservoir cover. Squeeze lever: must feel firm, not spongy. A spongy lever means air remains — repeat the bleed.

6

Reinstall pads and wheel

Remove bleed block. Reinstall pads (arrow points in wheel rotation direction). Reinstall wheel. Check pad-to-rotor clearance: 0.2–0.3 mm each side. Test by riding slowly.

ℹ️
Bleed all 4 brakes one at a time. Never leave a brake partially bled overnight — mineral oil absorbs moisture from the air and you will need to redo it.

Troubleshooting

Systematic diagnosis of the most common assembly and running issues.

SymptomMost Likely CauseFix
Chain skips / jumpsWrong chain length, stiff link, or tensioner not engagedInspect for stiff link (flex chain sideways — stiff spots visible). Re-measure length. Check tensioner spring is engaged.
Suspension binds on compressionWishbone pivot bolt over-tightened with arm twistedLoosen bolt, let arm relax to natural position, re-torque.

Maintenance Schedule

A maintained Armadillo lasts decades. A neglected one fails at the worst moment.

Check brake pad beddingPads bed into rotor surface during first few uses
Listen for any new sounds during rideAny new click or creak = a loose fastener. Find it now, not on the road.

Every Month / 200 km

TaskNotes
Lubricate both chainsWet lube in wet conditions, dry wax in dry. Apply, wait 5 min, wipe excess.
Check chain stretchUse a chain checker tool. Replace at 0.5% wear. A worn chain destroys sprockets.
Check tyre pressureInflate to sidewall rating. Under-inflation causes pinch flats.
Brake lever feel checkAny sponginess = re-bleed needed.
Inspect brake padsReplace when pad material is under 1 mm thick.
Inspect bellows on all rod endsReplace any cracked or missing bellow immediately. Exposed ball joints corrode within weeks.
Loose bolt checkLightly tap each visible bolt head — a hollow sound means it's loose.

Loctite Colour Guide

Loctite 222 — Red/Purple (Weak)
Brake adapters, small bolts, light-duty fasteners. Removable by hand tools. Cure: 30 min working, 24h full.
Loctite 243 — Blue (Medium)
Seat rails, frame bolts, general purpose. Removable with standard tools + heat. Cure: 30 min working, 24h full.
When is Loctite "cured"?

After 30 min: the Loctite has set enough that the bolt won't vibrate loose immediately. After 24h at room temperature (20°C): fully cured — maximum strength. Do not stress Loctite-fastened joints within 30 min of application.

Assembly Order reminder
BOLT→WASHER→BUSH→PART→BUSH→WASHER→NUT

The Two-Wrench Technique

Most bolts in this build require holding the bolt still while turning the nut — if you only use one spanner the bolt will just spin. This is the fundamental technique for tightening suspension joints.

Blue spanner: holds bolt head still. Red spanner: tightens the nut clockwise. Both hands needed.

Glossary of Key Terms

Kingpin

The vertical spindle each wheel pivots around — like a car's steering knuckle. Upper and lower wishbones connect to it.

Wishbone

A-shaped suspension arm connecting frame to kingpin. 8 total: 1 upper + 1 lower per corner.

Rod End

Threaded shaft with a ball-and-socket joint. LEFT thread = tightens counter-clockwise. RIGHT thread = tightens clockwise (normal).

Bellow

Rubber boot sealing grease inside a rod end. Insert rod end through the SIDE SLIT, not the end hole.

Distance Bush

Hollow metal spacer cylinder on a bolt. Prevents over-clamping and ensures the rod end ball can still move freely after tightening.

Nylon Stop Nut

Self-locking nut with a nylon insert that grips the thread. Do NOT reuse — the nylon deforms on first use.

Silent Block

Rubber-bonded bushing in wishbone ends. The bolt MUST pass through the rubber centre — never bypass it.

Parallel Key

Small rectangular metal bar that locks two rotating parts (axle + sprocket) together. Always grease before inserting.

Cardan Shaft

Universal-joint driveshaft connecting motor output to rear wheel axle. Has a spring inside — do NOT forget it when assembling.

Bafang Max

The 250W mid-drive electric motor. Torque-sensing — feels how hard you pedal and provides proportional assist.

Rohloff Speedhub

14-speed internal gear hub. Shift via twist-grip on handlebar. Change gears only while pedalling lightly. Oil change every 5,000 km.

Freewheel

Ratchet mechanism between the motor and the chain. Allows you to coast without back-driving the motor. Two bearings inside a housing.

Front Boom

The forward-extending tube on the main frame that holds the pedals and chainring. The Bafang motor is at the rear of this tube.

IS Brake Adapter

"International Standard" brake caliper mount. A tab bolted to the kingpin that the caliper clamps to. Always use Loctite 222 on these bolts.

Critical reference
Left / Right Identification Guide
Many parts come in mirrored left/right pairs. Installing the wrong side in the wrong position causes geometry problems that are hard to diagnose. Use this guide every time you pick up a mirrored part.
The #1 build mistake is installing left/right mirrored parts on the wrong side. Always check before bolting down.

LEFT SIDE

= the rider's left hand

When sitting in the bike facing forward, the LEFT side is your left hand side — which is the same side as the Rohloff gear hub and the twist-grip shifter.

RIGHT SIDE

= the rider's right hand

When sitting in the bike facing forward, the RIGHT side is your right hand side — the drive side. The Bafang chainring, chain and main drivetrain are on this side.

How to Check a Mirrored Part

Find the brake tab. It always points in a specific direction — match to diagram.

Hold both parts side-by-side. L and R are mirror images — bolt holes on opposite sides.

Always check the part code. Matched pairs usually have consecutive numbers.

After identifying each mirrored pair, label them with masking tape ("FL", "FR", "RL", "RR") before you start assembling. Remove the tape as each part is installed.

Know Your Parts

Before you touch a single bolt, pick up each part, read its code, and match it to a card below. Build up this mental picture first — it saves hours of confusion later.

0074 Main Frame (Chassis)

What it is: The backbone of the whole bike. A welded aluminium/steel tube structure that everything else bolts onto.

Identify it: Longest single piece in the kit. Has four large circular holes at the corners where suspension arms attach. Also has a central tube for the bottom bracket (pedal axle).

Handle with care: Do not drop — frame alignment is set at the factory. Any bend means the wheels won't track straight.

0068 Front Boom (Pedal Tube)

What it is: A telescoping tube that holds the bottom bracket (pedals) out in front of the rider. Think of it as the "pedal arm" of the bike.

Identify it: Long round tube, narrower than the main frame. One end has a clamp/pinch collar; the other end has threads for the bottom bracket shell.

Adjustment: Slide in or out to set saddle-to-pedal distance. Tighten clamp bolt to 8–10 Nm. Must be horizontal (use a spirit level).

⚠️ Never ride with the clamp bolt loose — the boom can slide forward under load and you will lose control of the pedals.
0020 / 0021 Kingpin Assembly (Front Steering Pivot)

What it is: A vertical bolt that acts as the steering pivot for each front wheel. The wheel assembly rotates around this bolt when you steer.

Identify it: Gold/yellow bolt, ~150mm long, with a special shaped head. Used ONLY on front corners (×2 total).

0020 = Left kingpin (check thread direction). 0021 = Right kingpin.

Critical: Grease the bearing surfaces before assembly. Torque to spec — too tight = stiff steering; too loose = wheel wobble.

0072/0073/0075/0076 Wishbone Suspension Arms (×8 total)

What it is: A triangular metal arm that connects each wheel to the frame. Each corner of the bike has TWO wishbones — one upper and one lower. This is the "double wishbone" suspension system.

Identify it: V-shaped or triangular steel arm with three holes: two at the frame end (wide apart) and one at the wheel end (narrow, for kingpin/axle).

8 arms total: 4 upper + 4 lower, one per wheel corner. They are NOT interchangeable — upper and lower are different sizes.

Label each arm with masking tape (e.g., "FL Upper", "FR Lower") as soon as you unpack them.
0017 / 0018 / 0019 Rod Ends (Pivot Ball Joints)

What it is: A ball joint on a threaded rod. One end screws into the wishbone arm; the ball end pivots freely in all directions. This allows the suspension to move without binding.

Three types:

• 0017 — M10 thread, for suspension arms (larger, heftier)

• 0018 — M8 LEFT-HAND thread (groove marked on hex), for steering

• 0019 — M8 RIGHT-HAND thread (no groove), for steering

⚠️ LEFT-HAND thread (0018) tightens COUNTER-CLOCKWISE. Mark it with blue tape to avoid stripping it. You can identify it by the groove cut into the hex flats.
0022 Bellows (Rubber Dust Boots)

What it is: A rubber accordion boot that wraps around a rod end to keep dirt and water out. Without it, the ball joint wears out quickly.

Identify it: Small black rubber sleeve with a slit along one side. Looks like a miniature accordion.

How to fit: Do NOT try to push the rod end through the end hole. Instead: squash the bellow flat → open the side slit → slip the rod end shank into the slit → release → bellow wraps around naturally.

Tip: A tiny smear of dish soap on the rubber makes it slide on much easier.
DR8 / DR10 Distance Bushes (Spacer Cylinders)

What it is: A short metal cylinder (spacer) that goes inside the wishbone arm hole before the bolt is inserted. It limits how far you can tighten the bolt, preventing you from crushing the silent block rubber.

DR8 = for M8 bolts (smaller, ~8mm inner diameter). DR10 = for M10 bolts (larger, ~10mm inner diameter).

Assembly order: Wishbone hole → distance bush → silent block → bolt. The bush and silent block sit concentrically.

⚠️ Missing a distance bush = over-tightening = crushed rubber = suspension binding or failure.
0026 / 0027 Silent Blocks (Rubber Frame Bushings)

What it is: A rubber-and-metal sandwich bushing that is pressed into the frame (chassis) holes at the wishbone pivot points. The rubber absorbs vibration and small misalignments.

Identify it: Short cylinder with a rubber outer sleeve and a metal inner sleeve. About the diameter of a 10p coin.

Fitting: Press into frame hole by hand first, then use a bench vice or large socket to seat it fully flush. The bolt MUST pass through the metal inner sleeve — never through the rubber.

If the silent block spins in the hole, it wasn't pressed in fully. Re-seat it before bolting.
0142 Shock Absorbers (×4)

What it is: A hydraulic damper (like a car shock absorber) that cushions bumps. One at each wheel corner (4 total).

Identify it: A cylindrical unit about 150–200mm long with a metal eye (loop) at each end. When you push the two eyes together it resists — that's the oil damping.

Orientation: Damper body faces UP; piston rod faces DOWN. Never mount upside-down — oil will pool in the wrong chamber and damping fails.

Mounting: Eye bolts through the wishbone and frame bracket. Use the supplied M8 bolts + nylon-stop nuts. Torque 25 Nm.

0192 Cardan Shaft (Universal-Joint Driveshaft ×2)

What it is: A two-piece driveshaft with a cross-shaped universal joint in the middle. Transmits pedal power from the motor/chainring to the rear wheel sprocket even when the suspension moves up and down.

Identify it: Two metal tubes joined by a cross-shaped spider joint in the middle. Has splined ends that slide onto axles. About 300–400mm long.

CRITICAL — internal spring: Inside one of the tubes is a small coil spring. Before connecting the shaft, verify this spring is present. Without it, the shaft will rattle and can disconnect under load.

2 shafts: One per rear wheel (left and right).

⚠️ Always check spring is present before installing. Shake the shaft — you should hear/feel a slight spring resistance at the mid-joint.
6×6×20mm Parallel Keys (Woodruff Keys)

What it is: A small rectangular metal prism that fits into matching slots cut into both a shaft (axle) and a hub (sprocket/gear). It prevents the sprocket from spinning on the axle.

Identify it: Tiny rectangular steel bar, exactly 6mm × 6mm × 20mm. Looks like a small piece of square bar stock.

Installation: Clean both slots → apply a film of grease → press key into axle slot → align hub slot → push hub onto axle. The key should be a snug sliding fit, not sloppy.

⚠️ A dry or loose key will hammer back and forth and cause wear. Grease prevents fretting corrosion.
0008 + 0069 + 0088 Freewheel Assembly

What it is: The freewheel lets you coast without pedalling. It consists of a cylindrical housing (0088) with two press-fit bearings (0008) mounted on an axle (0069). The internal ratchet mechanism allows the chain to drive the wheel forward but spin freely when you stop pedalling.

Pre-assembly (do this before Phase 4):

1. Press bearing 0008 into housing 0088 with grease — use a socket the same size as the bearing outer race

2. Repeat for second bearing on opposite side

3. Slide axle 0069 through both bearings

4. Secure with circlip (check it seats in groove)

⚠️ Bearings must be pressed in straight. A tilted bearing will bind and wear out quickly. Use a vice with flat jaws.
0015 Bafang Max Mid-Drive Motor (250W)

What it is: The electric motor that assists your pedalling. It fits into the bottom bracket shell of the front boom (replaces the normal pedal axle). It is a "torque-sensing" motor — it only adds power when you push harder on the pedals.

Identify it: Large round unit (~120mm diameter) with a central threaded spindle, a cable exiting the top, and two flat sides for a BB tool. Branded "Bafang".

Sides: The drive side (right, where chain goes) has a chainring spider. The non-drive side (left) has the cable exit.

Thread size: 68mm BSA (standard British bike thread) or 73mm depending on frame spec.

0003 / 0013 Chainrings (42T Front + 38T Freewheel)

What they are: Toothed rings that the chain runs on. The 42T front chainring (0003) is bolted to the Bafang motor spider. The 38T ring (0013) mounts on the freewheel assembly at the rear and drives the Rohloff hub via a second chain.

Identify them: Steel or aluminium rings with teeth around the outer edge. The 42T is slightly larger (more teeth) than the 38T.

Mounting bolts: Use chainring bolts with Loctite 243. Tighten in a star pattern (like wheel lug nuts) to 8–10 Nm. Check all 5 bolts are tight after first ride.

Count the teeth if unsure which is which: 42 teeth = bigger = goes on the motor (front).
0006 / 0007 Chain Idler Pulleys

What they are: Small toothed pulley wheels mounted on the frame that guide the chain around corners and keep it from drooping or rubbing the frame.

Two idlers: 0006 (upper, larger) guides the chain over the top. 0007 (lower, smaller) guides the return run under the frame.

Bearing inside: Each idler spins on a sealed bearing. When fitting, make sure the bolt passes through the bearing inner race — do not overtighten or the bearing will bind.

After chain installation, rotate pedals slowly and watch each idler. All should spin freely with no chain rubbing.
Rohloff Speedhub 500/14 14-Speed Internal Gear Hub

What it is: A premium 14-speed gearbox built into the rear wheel hub. All gears are sealed inside the hub — no derailleur, no exposed cables, weather-proof.

Identify it: Large chrome/silver hub on the rear wheel, noticeably bigger than a normal hub. Has TWO cable exit ports on the drive side. Stamped "Rohloff Speedhub 500/14".

Key features: Bayonet axle ends (twist to remove wheel), oil-bath lubrication (change oil annually), 2 shift cables (both needed), twist-grip shifter only.

Sprocket torque: 40 Nm with thread-lock compound. This is the highest torque in the build — use a proper torque wrench.

Rohloff Twist Shifter Handlebar Gear Shifter

What it is: The gear-change control on the handlebar. Rotate the rubber grip to shift up or down through 14 gears. Has a small window showing the current gear number.

Identify it: Oversized handlebar grip with a numbered window (1–14). Two thin shift cables exit from the body.

Installation: Clamp onto handlebar with the two included M3 bolts. Run two shift cables (different lengths) from shifter to Rohloff hub. Cable tension is critical — see Phase 5.

Shift only when pedalling: Rohloff requires the drivetrain to be under slight load to shift cleanly. Do not shift while stationary.

Battery Pack + Mount (0047) Lithium Battery & Mount

What it is: The lithium-ion rechargeable battery that powers the Bafang motor. Mounts to the frame via bracket 0047.

Voltage: Must match the motor voltage exactly (typically 36V or 48V). Mixing voltages will damage the motor controller.

Connectors: XT60 (yellow plastic, gold pins) or XT90 (larger). Red = positive (+), Black = negative (−). NEVER connect reversed.

Position: Mount as low as possible on the frame for better handling. Avoid mounting high up — raises the centre of gravity.

⚠️ Never short the battery terminals. Keep the battery away from water and fire. If the battery swells, do NOT charge it — dispose of it safely at a battery recycling point.
0082 Chain Tensioner

What it is: A spring-loaded arm with a small pulley wheel that presses against the chain to keep it taut. Prevents the chain from skipping or derailing when the suspension moves.

Identify it: A pivot arm with a spring inside the pivot and a small toothed pulley at the end. Mounts on a frame tab near the bottom bracket.

Setup: Install with spring in the tensioned position (arm pressing into chain). There should be 5–10mm of chain sag when measured midway between sprockets with the tensioner set. Too tight = chain breakage; too loose = skipping.

0364 / 0554 / 0361 Lights (Head / Tail / Indicators)

What they are: The lighting system. The Armadillo has integrated LED lights powered by the main battery.

0364 Headlight: Front-facing white LED. Mounts on the nose of the fairing or front boom. Two-wire connector (red/black).

0554 Taillight: Rear-facing red LED. Mounts at the rear of the frame. Also two-wire.

0361 Indicators: Amber flashing LEDs, one each side front and rear (4 total). Controlled by handlebar-mounted button.

Wiring: All lights connect to the main wiring loom. Follow the colour code — white=headlight, red/amber=indicators. See Wiring Guide section.

0574 / 0575 Front & Rear Bumpers

What they are: Rubber or plastic protective strips that bolt onto the front (0574) and rear (0575) of the frame. They absorb minor impacts and protect the frame from scratches when parking.

Installation: Last step before final checks. 4× M6 bolts per bumper. Tighten to 8 Nm. No thread-lock needed — bumpers may need to be removed for service.

Inspect periodically: If a bumper is cracked or loose, replace it. A missing bumper allows direct frame contact with walls/posts.

Tektro Hydraulic Disc Hydraulic Disc Brake System (×4)

What it is: Hydraulic brakes with a metal rotor bolted to each wheel and a caliper that squeezes brake pads onto the rotor. Operated by levers on the handlebar.

Fluid type: MINERAL OIL ONLY — Tektro hydraulic systems use mineral oil, NOT DOT brake fluid. DOT fluid will destroy the seals within hours. The bottle is labelled.

Rotor bolt torque: 4–5 Nm with Loctite 222 (red, weak-strength). The bolts are small — do not overtighten or they will strip.

Pad clearance: 0.2–0.3mm gap each side. Adjust caliper position (loosen the two mounting bolts, centre the caliper, re-tighten).

Steering Rods + Turnbuckle Toe Adjustment Linkage

What it is: The linkage rods that connect the steering rack to each front wheel. The turnbuckle (central barrel with left and right threads) allows you to adjust the length of the rod to set wheel alignment (toe).

Identify it: Two rod ends (ball joints) connected by a threaded barrel. Turning the barrel clockwise lengthens the rod; counter-clockwise shortens it.

Toe setting: Front wheels should be parallel (0mm toe) or very slightly toe-in (1–2mm). Measured between the front edges of the tyres vs rear edges.

Lock it: Once alignment is set, tighten the lock nut hard against the turnbuckle barrel. If the lock nut loosens in use, the wheel alignment drifts and the bike pulls to one side.

Fastener Guide

Bolts, nuts, and washers are not all the same. Here is what you will encounter and how to handle each type.

Standard Hex Bolt — most common. Turn clockwise to tighten. Use the matching size spanner on the hex head.

Nylon Stop Nut — has a nylon ring inside that grips the thread and prevents loosening. ONE USE ONLY — once removed, throw it away and use a new one. Never re-use.

Left-Hand Thread — tightens counter-clockwise. Identified by a groove cut into the hex flats. Used on some rod ends (0018) and the Rohloff sprocket. Mark with blue tape!

Flat Washer — always use one under the bolt head AND under the nut. Without washers the bolt pulls into the aluminium and loosens over time.

Spring Washer — slightly dished/split ring. Bites into the bolt and surface to resist vibration. Used on certain frame bolts where no nylon-stop nut is specified.

Thread-Lock Guide

Loctite 222 (Purple/Red — weak)

Use on: brake rotor bolts, brake adapter bolts, small fasteners (M5 or smaller) where you need easy removal.

Cure time: 24 hours full cure, 30 min handling.

Loctite 243 (Blue — medium)

Use on: seat rail bolts, chainring bolts, Rohloff sprocket, general M6–M10 fasteners.

Cure time: 24 hours full cure, 1 hour handling.

No thread-lock

Use on: suspension pivot bolts (nylon-stop nuts instead), wheel axle nuts, bumper bolts (serviceable parts).

⚠️ Apply thread-lock to CLEAN, DRY threads. Grease or oil will prevent it curing. One small drop is enough — a single drop wicked into the thread is sufficient.

Phase 1 Verify & Clean All Parts

⏱ Estimated time: 1–2 hours

This phase has no assembly. Its entire purpose is to prevent expensive mistakes later. Do not skip it.

1

Unpack and inventory everything

Parts list (previous section) Marker pen Masking tape

Lay every part out on a large clean surface — a carpet or cardboard sheet protects parts from scratching. Go through the Parts List row by row. Put a tick next to each part as you find it.

For matched pairs (left/right wishbones, etc.), label them with masking tape: "FL Upper", "FR Upper", "RL Upper", "RR Upper" and the same for lower arms. Do this NOW while you can tell them apart easily.

Why: A missing part discovered mid-build means disassembly and waiting for parts. Catching it now takes 2 minutes.
2

Inspect the main frame for damage

0074 Main Frame Bright light or torch

Examine every weld, tube junction, and hole. Look for:

• Cracks in the metal (especially at weld joints)

• Bent tubes (sight down each tube like a rifle barrel)

• Stripped or cross-threaded bolt holes

• Burrs or sharp edges (file smooth if found)

⚠️ If you find any crack, stop. Do not build on a cracked frame. Contact Velove for a replacement.
Why: A damaged frame is invisible inside a finished build and will fail dangerously.
3

Clean all threaded holes

Thread tap set (matching bolt sizes) Wire brush or stiff brush Compressed air (optional)

Run a matching tap through every bolt hole in the frame to clear any paint, powder coat, or machining debris from the threads. Even new frames often have partially blocked threads from the coating process.

After tapping, blow out or brush out the swarf. A partly blocked thread will make you think a bolt is tight when it isn't — a dangerous false sense of security.

Thread taps can be bought cheaply as a set from any hardware store. You need M5, M6, M8, M10 sizes.
4

Press silent blocks into frame

0026/0027 Silent Blocks (×8) Bench vice or large socket Flat piece of wood (pressing plate)
1. Hold silent block over frame hole — rubber side in
2. Press by hand until it starts to enter
3. Use vice or socket to push flush
4. Check: must be fully seated, flush with frame surface
⚠️ Do not hammer silent blocks directly — use a flat surface or socket to distribute the pressing force evenly or the rubber will tear.
5

Grease all rod end threads

0017/0018/0019 Rod Ends Copper grease or anti-seize

Apply a thin smear of copper grease or anti-seize to the threaded shanks of all rod ends. This prevents thread galling (metal welding together under load) and makes future adjustments possible.

⚠️ Do NOT grease the ball joint portion — grease attracts dirt. Only grease the threaded shank.

Phase 2 Suspension Pre-Assembly

⏱ Estimated time: 3–4 hours

Build all four suspension corners: lower wishbones, upper wishbones, and shock absorbers. The bike will not stand on its own wheels until the end of this phase.

1

Fit rod ends into wishbone arms

All 8 wishbone arms 0017 Rod Ends (×16) 0022 Bellows (×16) Spanner 15mm + 17mm
1. Slide bellow onto rod end shank (through SIDE SLIT, not end hole)
2. Screw rod end into wishbone arm end — hand tight first
3. Final: two-wrench technique — 25 Nm
4. Pull bellow over joint body to cover it
⚠️ The rod end must be installed straight (not cross-threaded). If it requires more than light finger pressure to start, stop — the thread is mis-aligned. Back it out and try again.
2

Mount lower wishbones to frame

4× Lower Wishbone Arms DR10 Distance Bushes (×8) M10 Bolts + Nylon-Stop Nuts Torque wrench
1. Work on ONE corner at a time (e.g., Front-Left first)
2. Insert DR10 distance bush into silent block hole
3. Align lower wishbone with frame bracket
4. Insert M10 bolt through frame → distance bush → wishbone pivot
5. Fit NEW nylon-stop nut on other end
6. Torque: 35 Nm
7. Repeat for all 4 lower arms (front and rear)
Leave the wishbones slightly loose (finger-tight) on BOTH frame pivot bolts until the wheel assembly is also fitted. Tighten to full torque only once all three attachment points are connected. This prevents binding.
3

Mount upper wishbones to frame

4× Upper Wishbone Arms (0072/0073/0075/0076) DR8 Distance Bushes (×8) M8 Bolts + Nylon-Stop Nuts

Same procedure as lower wishbones but using M8 hardware and DR8 distance bushes. Upper arms attach to the UPPER frame bracket, directly above the lower arm attachment point.

Torque: 25 Nm once all attachments are connected.

⚠️ Upper and lower wishbones are NOT the same length. Upper arms are shorter. If you mix them up, the wheel geometry will be wrong and the suspension will bind.
4

Fit wheels to wishbone hubs

0397 Wheels (×4) Axle bolts M10 35 Nm torque wrench

Connect the rod end (wheel end of wishbone) to the wheel hub carrier. Insert the hub pivot bolt through upper wishbone rod end → hub carrier → lower wishbone rod end. Fit nylon-stop nuts and torque to 35 Nm.

Once the wheel assembly is connected, go back and torque all wishbone frame-pivot bolts (from Steps 2 and 3) to their final values.

5

Install shock absorbers

0142 Shock Absorbers (×4) M8 Eye Bolts + Nylon-Stop Nuts 25 Nm torque wrench
1. Orient shock: damper body UP, piston rod DOWN
2. Line up upper eye with frame bracket
3. Insert M8 bolt through eye — torque 25 Nm
4. Line up lower eye with lower wishbone bracket
5. Insert M8 bolt through eye — torque 25 Nm
6. Check: shock should compress slightly when you push down on the corner
Why body-up: The oil in the damper must be in the lower chamber (around the piston). Inverting it puts oil in the wrong place and the damper becomes a useless air spring.
6

Suspension bounce test

With all four corners assembled, push down firmly on each corner of the bike. The suspension should:

✓ Compress smoothly without binding or scraping

✓ Rebound slowly (damper doing its job) — NOT spring back instantly

✓ Return to the same height each time

If a corner binds, check that all wishbone pivot bolts were tightened with the arm in its natural resting position (not twisted). If a corner springs back instantly, the damper may be inverted.

Phase 3 Front Kingpins & Steering

⏱ Estimated time: 2–3 hours

Install the front wheel steering pivots (kingpins) and connect the steering linkage. After this phase the front wheels will steer.

1

Grease and insert kingpins

0020 Left Kingpin 0021 Right Kingpin Bearing grease (lithium-based) Rubber mallet
1. Pack all bearing surfaces with grease — use a finger or brush
2. Insert kingpin from TOP into steering knuckle/hub carrier
3. Tap gently with rubber mallet to seat through upper bearing
4. Continue through lower bearing
5. Thread top and bottom nuts — left kingpin uses left-hand thread
6. Torque top nut 30 Nm, bottom nut 30 Nm
7. Verify: wheel pivots smoothly side-to-side with no stiffness or play
⚠️ 0020 Left Kingpin uses left-hand thread — tighten counter-clockwise. If you force it clockwise you will strip the thread. Verify before applying torque.
2

Install steering rack/crossbar

Steering rack assembly M8 mounting bolts

Bolt the central steering rack/crossbar to the frame centre bracket. This is the bar that links the steering input (handlebars) to both front wheels simultaneously. Torque the mounting bolts to 25 Nm.

Centre the rack exactly — there should be equal travel left and right. Measure from the rack centre to each frame side — these distances should be equal (within 2mm).
3

Install steering rod ends (0018 + 0019)

0018 Rod End M8 Left-thread (×1) 0019 Rod End M8 Right-thread (×1) Steering turnbuckle rods (×2) 0022 Bellows Two 15mm spanners

Screw one rod end into each end of both steering rods. Note: one rod uses 0018 (left-thread) on the rack end and 0019 (right-thread) on the wheel end — this is intentional to allow the turnbuckle to shorten or lengthen the rod by rotating the barrel.

Set initial length: screw both rod ends to equal engagement depth (about 15mm each). Full alignment adjustment happens in Phase 5.

⚠️ 0018 tightens counter-clockwise. A groove on the hex flats identifies it. Never mix 0018 and 0019 on the same rod end — one rod needs one of each.
4

Connect steering rods to kingpins and rack

Bolt the rod end ball joints to the kingpin steering arm and to the rack. Use M8 bolts with flat washers and nylon-stop nuts. Torque 20 Nm. Leave the turnbuckle lock nuts loose — you will adjust wheel alignment in Phase 5.

After connecting, turn the handlebars full lock left and full lock right. All steering rods should move smoothly without any cable or hose fouling. Trace all cables and hoses now to catch any routing problems early.

Phase 4 Freewheel, Bafang Motor & Drivetrain

⏱ Estimated time: 3–4 hours

Install the motor, freewheel, cardan shafts, and chain idlers. After this phase the drivetrain is mechanically complete.

1

Pre-assemble the freewheel unit

0088 Freewheel Housing 0008 Bearings (×2) 0069 Freewheel Axle Bearing grease Socket matching bearing OD Bench vice
1. Pack both bearings (0008) with grease — work grease between all balls
2. Place housing (0088) on the vice jaws, flat side down
3. Set first bearing squarely in housing hole — must be perfectly level
4. Use socket (same OD as bearing outer race) + vice to press in until flush
5. Flip housing — repeat for second bearing on opposite side
6. Slide axle (0069) through both bearings
7. Fit circlip into axle groove to retain it
8. Check: axle should spin smoothly, housing should NOT spin on axle
⚠️ If bearing is pressed in at an angle, it will bind and wear out within weeks. Take your time — get it perfectly level before pressing.
Why pre-assemble: Once the housing is on the bike, you cannot press bearings in square. This must be done on the bench.
2

Install Bafang Max motor into front boom

0015 Bafang Max 250W Motor 0068 Front Boom Bafang BB splined cup tool 17–18 Nm torque wrench Anti-seize grease (for threads)
1. Clean the BB shell (bottom bracket thread) in the front boom
2. Apply thin film of anti-seize to BB thread (NOT the motor itself)
3. Thread motor in from the drive side (right side, where chain goes)
4. Thread in by hand — if it binds, stop and re-align
5. Once fully threaded in, use BB tool to torque: 17–18 Nm
6. Route motor cable through boom clamp and out to controller position
The motor will only thread in one way — British Standard threading (BSA). If it feels very stiff, check you have the right thread specification for your boom (68mm or 73mm BSA).
3

Install chainring on Bafang motor

0003 Chainring 42T Chainring bolts (×5) Loctite 243 (blue) 8–10 Nm torque wrench
1. Align chainring spider holes with Bafang spider arms
2. Apply ONE small drop of Loctite 243 to each bolt thread
3. Thread in all 5 bolts by hand before tightening any
4. Tighten in STAR PATTERN (like wheel lug nuts) — not around in a circle
5. Final torque: 8–10 Nm on each bolt
6. Check ring runs true — spin by hand and watch for wobble (max 1mm)
4

Install cardan shafts (×2)

0192 Cardan Shafts (×2) 6×6×20mm Parallel Keys (×4) Copper grease
1. FIRST: shake each cardan shaft — verify internal spring is present (slight resistance at joint)
2. Clean axle key slots and cardan shaft key slots
3. Apply thin grease film to parallel key and both slots
4. Press parallel key into AXLE slot — should be snug, not sloppy
5. Align cardan shaft key slot with the key on axle
6. Push cardan shaft onto axle — key engages in both slots
7. Check: shaft should turn with axle, not spin freely on it
8. Fit retaining clip (if applicable) to keep shaft on axle
⚠️ NO internal spring = shaft will separate under load causing sudden loss of drive. ALWAYS verify spring before connecting. It's a 30-second check that prevents a dangerous failure.
5

Mount freewheel assembly

Freewheel assembly (pre-built in Step 1) 0013 Chainring 38T Frame mounting bolts

Bolt the complete freewheel assembly (housing + axle + bearings) to the frame bracket near the rear of the drivetrain. The 38T chainring (0013) mounts to the freewheel axle using a parallel key, same procedure as the cardan shaft.

The cardan shaft then connects the freewheel axle to the Rohloff hub axle (installed in Phase 5). Do not connect the cardan shaft to the Rohloff hub yet.

6

Install chain idler pulleys (0006 + 0007)

0006 Upper Idler 0007 Lower Idler M8 idler bolts 15 Nm torque wrench

Mount each idler pulley on its frame tab using the M8 bolt. Tighten to 15 Nm. After tightening, spin each idler by hand — it must spin freely. If it binds, back off the bolt slightly. The idler bearing must not be compressed.

The chain will be routed over and under these idlers in Phase 6. For now, just confirm they spin freely and are secure.
7

Install chain tensioner (0082)

0082 Chain Tensioner M8 mounting bolt

Bolt the chain tensioner to the designated frame tab. The spring arm should face toward the chain path (lower section between freewheel and rear chainring). Do not tension against the chain yet — the chain is installed in Phase 6.

Phase 5 Rohloff Hub, Steering Alignment & Brakes

⏱ Estimated time: 3–4 hours
1

Install Rohloff hub into rear wheel

Rohloff Speedhub 500/14 Rear wheel (right side, drive side) Rohloff sprocket tool 40 Nm torque wrench Thread-lock (medium strength)
1. Mount Rohloff hub into rear right wheel (it is the drive-side wheel)
2. Thread sprocket onto hub — it has LEFT-HAND thread (counter-clockwise to tighten)
3. Apply thread-lock to sprocket thread before final tightening
4. Use Rohloff sprocket tool to torque to 40 Nm
5. Engage bayonet axle ends in rear axle slots — twist to lock
6. Fit and torque wheel axle nuts: 35 Nm
⚠️ 40 Nm is the highest torque value in this build. Use a proper calibrated torque wrench. Under-torqued sprocket will unscrew in use and destroy the hub threading.
2

Connect cardan shaft to Rohloff hub

With the Rohloff hub installed in the wheel and the freewheel assembly already fitted, connect the cardan shaft (0192) between the freewheel axle and the Rohloff hub axle, following the same parallel key procedure used in Phase 4 Step 4.

⚠️ Double-check the internal spring is present in the cardan shaft AGAIN before connecting. It is easy to dislodge the spring when handling the shaft.
3

Route and connect Rohloff shift cables

Rohloff Twist Grip Shifter 2× Shift cables (inner wires) 2× Cable outer housing Cable end crimps (×4) Cable cutters
1. Mount twist-grip shifter on handlebar — clamp with M3 bolts, torque 3 Nm
2. Route cable outer housing from shifter to Rohloff hub (right side panel)
3. Rohloff has TWO cable ports — cable 1 (shorter) enters at the top port
4. Cable 2 (longer) enters at the lower port
5. Thread inner wires through housing and connect to hub bayonet connectors
6. Set tension: shift to gear 4 — cable should just be taut with no sag
7. Crimp end caps on all cut cable ends
8. Test shift through all 14 gears while pedalling slowly
Rohloff uses bayonet connectors for cable attachment — push and twist (like a light bulb). These allow the wheel to be removed without tools. If cables seem too short or long, you can adjust the barrel adjusters on the shifter housing.
⚠️ Cable length matters. Rohloff provides two cables of different lengths. Do not cut them to the same length or the cable routing will be wrong and one gear direction will not function.
4

Wheel alignment — set toe

Tape measure Two straight rulers or lengths of string Steering rod turnbuckles Two 15mm spanners
1. Set steering to straight-ahead (centred)
2. Measure FRONT edge tyre-to-tyre distance (both front wheels)
3. Measure REAR edge tyre-to-tyre distance (same two wheels)
4. Target: front measurement = rear measurement (0mm toe) or front 1–2mm LESS (slight toe-in)
5. Adjust: loosen turnbuckle lock nuts → turn barrel to change rod length → re-measure
6. Tighten lock nuts to 20 Nm when alignment is correct
Why toe matters: Incorrect toe causes tyres to scrub sideways while driving, wearing them out in days and making the bike unstable.
5

Install disc brake rotors

4× Disc rotors Torx T25 key Loctite 222 (red/purple weak) 4–5 Nm torque wrench

Each rotor has 6 bolts. Apply ONE drop of Loctite 222 to each bolt. Tighten in star pattern (same as chainring — opposite bolts first) to 4–5 Nm. Rotor bolts are SMALL — do not overtighten or they will strip.

After fitting, spin the wheel and look at the rotor edge-on. It should run perfectly true (flat). A wobbling rotor means an undertightened bolt. Re-torque in star pattern.
6

Mount brake calipers

4× Tektro hydraulic calipers Brake adaptor plates (if required) M6 caliper bolts Loctite 222 8–10 Nm torque wrench
1. If adapter plates are needed, mount them first with Loctite 222, torque 8–10 Nm
2. Mount caliper loosely on adapter/frame (do not tighten fully yet)
3. Insert a folded piece of card between pads to centre caliper over rotor
4. Squeeze brake lever firmly to push pads against the card
5. While holding lever: tighten caliper bolts to 8–10 Nm
6. Release lever, remove card, spin wheel — rotor must clear pads both sides
7

Connect brake hoses and bleed

Route hydraulic hoses from handlebar levers to each caliper. Secure with all frame clips. Refer to the Brake Bleeding section later in this manual for the full bleeding procedure.

⚠️ Tektro hydraulic brakes use MINERAL OIL only. Never use DOT fluid. Check the bottle label before opening it.

Phase 6 Front Boom, Chain, Battery & Final Assembly

⏱ Estimated time: 3–5 hours

The final phase. After this the bike is rideable. Take extra care — you are now checking everything you built in earlier phases.

1

Install and level the front boom (0068)

0068 Front Boom Spirit level M6 clamp bolt 8–10 Nm torque wrench
1. Insert the boom clamp end into the frame socket
2. Adjust slide length for rider leg length: sitting in seat, slight knee bend at pedal bottom
3. Place spirit level on top of the boom tube — adjust until perfectly horizontal
4. Tighten clamp bolt to 8–10 Nm
5. Verify boom does not rotate or slide — try to push it by hand
⚠️ A tilted boom changes the pedalling angle and causes knee strain. Always level it before tightening the clamp.
2

Install the main chain (front boom to freewheel)

Main chain Chain tool Master link Chain length guide (big-big method)
1. Route chain: over 42T front chainring → over upper idler 0006 → under lower idler 0007 → onto 38T freewheel chainring
2. Determine chain length (big-big+2 method below)
3. Cut chain to correct length using chain tool
4. Connect chain with master link — both plates must click fully
5. Tension the chain tensioner (0082) against the chain — 5–10mm sag midpoint
6. Spin pedals by hand and check: chain stays on sprockets, no rubbing
Big-Big+2 Chain Length Method:

1. Without the chain tensioner, wrap the chain around the LARGEST chainring (42T front) and LARGEST rear sprocket simultaneously

2. Pull the two ends together until snug

3. Add 2 links (= 1 complete link pair = 25.4mm)

4. This is the correct chain length

Err on the side of slightly long rather than slightly short. A too-short chain can break the derailleur (or in this case, jam the tensioner).
3

Install second chain (freewheel to Rohloff hub)

This shorter chain connects the 38T freewheel chainring output to the Rohloff hub sprocket. Route it directly between the two sprockets. Set chain length using the same big-big+2 method. Use a second master link to connect.

Spin the rear wheel by hand and pedal slowly (without weight on bike). Watch both chains — they should run smoothly with no chattering or jumping. Any jumping indicates wrong chain length or a stiff link.
4

Mount the battery

Lithium Battery Pack 0047 Battery Mount Bracket M6 bolts (×4)
1. Bolt mount bracket (0047) to frame at lowest suitable position — centred left-right
2. Slide or clip battery onto bracket — follow Velove's battery mounting direction
3. Secure any anti-rattle straps or clamps
4. Do NOT connect the power connector yet
⚠️ Keep battery as low as possible. Higher mounting raises the centre of gravity and makes the bike tip more easily in corners.
5

Connect the wiring loom

All wiring connectors Cable ties Wiring diagram (see Wiring section)

Follow the wiring colour code in the Wiring Guide section. Connect motor, display, brake cut-offs, throttle (if fitted), speed sensor, and lights. Secure all cables with cable ties — no cable should hang freely or touch a moving part.

Final step: connect the battery power connector (XT60/XT90). Red to red, black to black. You should see the display light up.

⚠️ Never connect power with the motor connectors reversed. Always match colours before connecting. A reversed motor connection will damage the controller immediately.
6

Install lights

0364 Headlight 0554 Taillight 0361 Indicators (×4) M5 mounting bolts

Mount headlight on the front of the boom or nose. Mount taillight on the rear of the frame. Mount indicators on each side (front-left, front-right, rear-left, rear-right). Connect each to the wiring loom. Torque mounting bolts to 3–4 Nm — plastic brackets crack easily.

Turn on the ignition and test each light individually. Verify indicators flash (not stay on). Verify headlight direction is forward (not up into rider's eyes).

7

Install front and rear bumpers

0574 Front Bumper 0575 Rear Bumper 4× M6 bolts per bumper 8 Nm torque wrench

Bolt front bumper to the nose of the frame. Bolt rear bumper to the tail of the frame. Torque all bolts to 8 Nm. No thread-lock — these are serviceable parts.

8

Final static checks before first power-on

Work through this checklist before riding:

✅ All suspension bolts torqued (check with torque wrench)

✅ All 4 wheels spin freely by hand without rubbing

✅ Brake pads not dragging (spin each wheel — no brake drag)

✅ Chain tension correct on both chains (5–10mm sag)

✅ All cables and hoses secured and away from moving parts

✅ Battery connector secure and waterproof

✅ Steering moves smoothly from lock to lock

✅ All lights working

✅ Tyre pressure correct (check sidewall for rated PSI)

Do the bounce test again: Push down on each corner. All 4 should rebound at the same speed. If one corner is stiff, re-check that wishbone bolt was not overtightened.

Brake Bleeding (Tektro Hydraulic)

⚠️ MINERAL OIL ONLY. Never use DOT fluid in Tektro brakes. DOT fluid destroys the seals immediately and the brakes will fail. The bottle will be labelled "Mineral Oil" or "Hydraulic Mineral Oil".

What you need

Tektro bleed kit (syringe + tube), Tektro mineral oil, 8mm hex key, flat-head screwdriver, paper towels, latex gloves.

1

Prepare the caliper end

Remove wheel. Remove brake pads and insert a bleed block (or folded card) between the pistons to stop them being pushed out. Locate the bleed nipple on the caliper body.

2

Prepare the lever end

Rotate the handlebar so the brake lever reservoir is level (horizontal). Remove the reservoir cover and bleed screw. The reservoir is usually under a rubber cover on the lever body.

3

Fill and push from caliper

Fill a syringe with mineral oil (no air bubbles — tap syringe and push air out first). Attach syringe tube to caliper bleed nipple. Open nipple 1/4 turn. Push oil slowly upward through the hose — air bubbles will rise to the lever reservoir.

4

Watch the reservoir

Continue pushing oil until no more bubbles emerge at the reservoir end. Top up the reservoir as needed — it must not run dry or you pull air back in.

5

Close and test

Close caliper nipple first (before removing syringe). Replace reservoir cover. Squeeze lever — it should feel firm, not spongy. A spongy lever means air is still in the system — repeat the bleed.

6

Reinstall pads and wheel

Remove bleed block. Reinstall brake pads (check pad orientation — arrow points in wheel rotation direction). Reinstall wheel. Check pad-to-rotor clearance (0.2–0.3mm each side). Test brake by riding slowly.

Bleed all 4 brakes one at a time. Never leave a brake partially bled overnight — oil will absorb moisture and you'll need to redo it.
